RCOS Collaboration

This semester we are collaborating with the Rensselaer Polytechnic Institute near Albany, New York. We are bringing one of their open source projects called YACS (Yet Another Course Scheduler) to NYU. Currently the NYU team is working on converting the Albert data to “YACS form”. Along with that there is work to be done on frontend and backend so YACS can be adopted for NYU. The team is looking for more members. If you would like to get involved please email bradley.brecher@nyu.edu.

Soccer Physics Unity Game

Mentor: EJ Kim
EJ’s team meets twice a week.

EJ and his team are recreating the classic 2D Soccer Physics game using Unity. In this game, players are controlled by a single button and the oly rule that applies is physics!

So far, EJ has taught his team the basics of Unity. Currently, EJ’s team is working together in smaller groups (Game play, UI, Player Control), to lay the foundation of the game. In the future, EJ hopes to customize the classic game with quirky features!

Seek Quench

Mentor: David Klein
His project is entirely virtual and will kick off during the summer of 2018, continuing through fall, and concluding at the end of the year. Follow the repository for the latest updates.

Classic bioinformatic sequence alignment algorithms implemented into a simple open source web app. The project is named Seek Quench because it sounds like sequence and we are trying to quench answers that people are seeking about their sequences.
